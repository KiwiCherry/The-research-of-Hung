

"We propose a mathematical framework in which the computational substrate is restructured to treat convolution expressions—specifically, sums and compositions of $N$ general convolution operations ($\sum(a_i * a_j)$ terms)—as primitive topological units rather than as intermediate representations requiring materialization into scalar outputs. In this framework, the convolution expression itself carries inherent structural meaning via functional isomorphism, bypassing the conventional pipeline of (1) computing $N$ scalar results, (2) embedding these scalars in an algebraic structure, and (3) deriving applications.Instead, functional intent and applicability are mapped directly from the convolution structure using zero intermediate scalar evaluations ($O(0)$ scalar operations). This is achieved via a representation-to-action mapping that exploits the intrinsic algebraic properties of convolution (associativity, distributivity, and closure under composition) as a native physical or topological state. By deliberately restricting the scope to general convolutions, we respect information-theoretic boundaries—avoiding the undecidability of universal systems while preserving dimensionality.The framework is intentionally decoupled from forced embedding into existing scalar-based algebraic paradigms, treating convolution topology as a foundational language in its own right. To visualize the paradigm shift: Instead of expending energy to calculate $N$ scalar outputs for $N$ convolution operations, the un-evaluated convolution topology exists within a specialized computational environment where its mere structural presence perfectly isomorphic to the desired functional outcome. This effectively renders intermediate scalar computation obsolete, replacing it with a single terminal projection into physical actuation." - Hung Dinh Phu Dang
